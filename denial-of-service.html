Denial of Service (DoS)

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack aims to make a service unavailable to its intended users by overwhelming it with a flood of illegitimate requests. This can lead to reduced performance or complete shutdown of the targeted service.
Types of DoS Attacks:

  • Volume-Based Attacks: These attacks aim to consume the bandwidth of the target. Example: UDP floods, ICMP floods.
  • Protocol Attacks: These target weaknesses in the protocols of the network. Example: SYN floods, Ping of Death.
  • Application Layer Attacks: These focus on exhausting server resources. Example: HTTP floods that aim to overwhelm web servers.

Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS): A more sophisticated form of DoS, where multiple compromised systems (often part of a botnet) launch an attack simultaneously, making it harder to mitigate.
Example: The 2016 DDoS attack on Dyn, a DNS service provider, disrupted major websites like Twitter, Netflix, and Reddit by overwhelming Dyn's servers with traffic from a large botnet.


Mitigation Strategies:
  • Implementing rate limiting to control the number of requests a server can handle.
  • Using firewalls and intrusion detection systems to filter out malicious traffic.
  • Employing content delivery networks (CDNs) to absorb and mitigate attack traffic.
  • Having a robust incident response plan to quickly react to attacks.
