Advanced Persistent Threats (APT)

An Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) is a prolonged and targeted cyberattack where an attacker gains unauthorized access to a network and remains undetected for an extended period. The primary goal is usually espionage or data theft rather than immediate damage.
Key Characteristics: Targeted attacks, stealthy presence, advanced techniques, continuous monitoring, and multiple stages of attack.
Phases: Reconnaissance, initial intrusion, establishing foothold, lateral movement, data exfiltration, and maintaining persistence.
Industries Targeted: Governments, financial institutions, healthcare, critical infrastructure, technology, research, and media.
Example: APT28 (Fancy Bear), a Russian cyber-espionage group, targeted the U.S. Democratic National Committee during the 2016 election, using phishing emails and malware to breach the system.
